10 principles of design

Dieter Rams

1. Good design is innovative

There is never an end to being innovative when technology is used to its fullest. As long as both work well hand in hand neither will become exhausted.

2. Good design makes a product useful

Design should be functional and well understood in order to be useful. Making a confusing product with a design that feels lost creates a lack of inspiration and influence.

3. Good design is aesthetic

Aesthetic works together with functionality. However finding something that is pleasing to the eye is different to each person. This goes along with appreciating an object visually and physically.

4. Good design makes a product understandable

The design of a product should be well made in order to recognize its function.

5. Good design is unobtrusive

Products are designed to have a purpose. Users will be more impacted if there is a purpose to the ideas being presented.

6. Good design is honest

Design should not manipulate people, but instead demonstrate its fullest potential.

7. Good design is long-lasting

Long-lasting design should take advantage of the high quality materials available today. Not only based on its design but its functionality as well.

8. Good design is thorough down to the last detail

Design is clear and concise, if not the product will not be taken seriously.

9. Good design is environmentally friendly

Having a sustainable product means that it has been thought out even after the object has been used and how it will continue to impact the world.

10. Good design is as little design as possible

Less is better. An overwhelming amount can affect the designs purpose and meaning.
